As part of my course work for the Starter League’s HTML/CSS class, I’m coding up this web page about myself.

I've lived in Chicago, IL since 2004. Before that, I grew up in suburban Minneapolis, MN. The Twin Cities are great, but Chicago is my home, and I can’t imagine living anywhere else.

Specifically, I live on the east side of Chicago’s Humboldt Park neighborhood, close enough to Ukrainian Village that the two areas blur. My neighbors are all hipsters, tiny Ukrainian grandmothers, and tattooed Puerto Rican motorcycle mechanics. It's a great place to be.

My favorite person in the world is a lady named Maddie, who I'm marrying next August. She won’t let me call her my ‘fiancé’ — she prefers ‘future wife’ because it’s funnier. We met in college while working at the student newspaper; she’s a graphic designer by trade and now works for a magazine publisher. She likes my dumb jokes and is just about as nerdy as I am, so I figured I should probably keep her around.

It was Maddie’s idea to get a pet rabbit a couple years ago. I was skeptical, but she really wanted one, so we adopted an albino miniature rex and named him Mister Sinister. He’s nothing but trouble (and he’s got a taste for beer and spicy food), but he’s got other redeeming qualities, so now we have a second rabbit, Brünhilde, to keep him company. She’s also trouble, but in different ways.

And about that beer — there’s plenty of it around our house because I brew my own with the help of a few friends. We set up a 20-gallon kettle on a propane burner in the garage every few weeks to make a new batch, and I’m told it’s pretty good. Maybe someday we’ll start an actual brewery.

When I’m not turning malt sugar into alcohol or working on keeping Mister S. away from the Sriracha bottle, I like to make stuff: I enjoy cooking, gardening, designing, and coding. I ride my bicycle everywhere I can, even if the weather sucks. I read a lot of classic superhero comics, and I really enjoy music. In 2010, I helped launch CHIRP Radio, a volunteer-driven local community radio station, where I put in more than five years as the founding music director. I’m not as involved anymore, but I’m still around as a member of the organization’s board of directors. And just to thoroughly sabotage my own indie cred, I DJ with a friend once a month at the Whistler in Logan Square, where we do a night of stupidly sincere ’90s pop and hip hop. We call ourselves The TTTTotally Dudes. I have no idea why.
